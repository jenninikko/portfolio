What I Bring

With a background in software and engineering environments, I focus on improving documentation quality, usability, and long-term maintainability.

🧭
User-first approach

Understanding user goals, pain points, and context shapes every documentation decision I make.

🧩
Product thinking

Treating docs as a product. I ask why a feature needs documentation, not just what to write.

📄
Clear and simple writing

I keep documentation organized, maintainable for others, and reusable across projects. I follow ASD-STE100 principles — short sentences, consistent terminology, no unnecessary complexity.

🏗
Information architecture

Before writing a single page, I map out how content connects, what users need at each step, and how the structure will hold up as the product grows. I design documentation structures that scale, from single guides to full knowledge bases, with the end user's journey in mind.

🔍
QA mindset

I test what I document. If something does not work as described, I flag it before it reaches the user.

👥
Working with developers

I sit in on standups, read code, and ask questions. Good docs come from understanding the product, not just the spec.
